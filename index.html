The Threshold

You have just arrived somewhere you didn’t know you were looking for.

This is the Threshold — the first space in Teams of Tomorrow®. If you have just completed the Regenerative Leader Scorecard, something in you was already asking questions.

This is where you begin to find out what they are.

The practice

This isn’t a programme. It’s a practice.

Three things. That’s it.

Every Monday

The Monday Prompt

One question in your inbox. Thirty seconds to read. Designed to stay with you all week — surfacing in quiet moments, interrupting assumptions. You don’t have to respond. Just let it work.

Once a month, live

The Monthly Master Class

60 to 75 minutes with Elly or Chris on one aspect of regenerative leadership. A real teaching, with room for dialogue at the end. Not a webinar, not a pitch. Recorded if you miss it — but the live room is where it happens.

Small and growing

The Practice Library

Six to eight short, applicable practices at launch — each a single page. No courses, no gated content. Each one can be used in a team meeting this week. The library grows slowly, by design.

Plainly

A word of honesty about what you won’t find here.

There are no badges, no leaderboards, no activity scores. No open chat, no pressure to post, no expectation of visible engagement.

There is no course map, no completion certificate, no curriculum to get through. If you are looking for a structured programme with measurable outputs, this is not it — and we’d rather say so now than waste your time.

What there is: one question a week worth sitting with. One substantial conversation a month worth showing up for. A small library of practices worth trying. That is enough. For now, it is everything.

Chris Jackson

We are not consultants or advisors. We ask questions. We hold space. We know that, given the right conditions, you’ll discover that you already know more than you think you do.

When it fits

When you’re ready to go further

The Threshold is yours to use at whatever depth suits you. There is no expectation of you to go further, and there is no pressure from us to do so.

Twice a year, a small cohort of leaders gather in the Circle — eight people, twelve weeks, doing the real work together. Threshold members hear about each intake one week before applications open publicly.

If that feels like a path you’d like to take, you’ll know when the time is right. You’ll have spent enough Monday mornings with the questions to know whether the answers matter enough to go deeper.
